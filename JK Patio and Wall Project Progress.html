    Originally Posted by MuirView Landscape
    Keep 'em comin' Jim. Kind of looks like the patio is sitting in a bit of a valley. Which way are your sending the water? Did you rent a laser level of some kind? I would set up 6 or 8 grade pins and criss cross string lines to check your heights. Look to get about an inch of fall per 8 to 10 feet to get the water moving.
    The ground is mostly level except for where the yard rises in the back (must be my pictures). Unfortunately I don't have a laser. I plan to grade this about 1 1/2" from back to front and for the layout this is what I did so far:
    • Started with the center of the circle and marked it with a spike
    • Set up 2 pins (front to back) so that when I tied a line between them it served as the diameter crossing the center spike.
    • Lined the string up at the front pin so that it was at grade level
    • Put a line level on the string, adjusted it, and then tied it up and marked it at grade level on the back pin.
    • Set up 2 pins on each side and ran a line so that it crossed over the spike and intersected the first line.
    • I also used a steel square (see picture below) to line up the second string, used the string level, and marked the pins at grade level.
    I then marked the back pin at 1 1/2" above the level mark and will use that as my guide for the height from back to front.

    I added 2 additional stakes (you can see them in the rain picture) that form a triangle from the back stake. I plan to use these to help with the height of the wall. It all probably sounds a little low-tech, but it's what I have. Because it is such a large area, and based on your comments above, it makes sense to add a couple more stakes to help out with the slope.

    I'm doing preliminary compacting for the lower lifts now but I'm getting close to the point where I need to get more exact.

    DON'T...the reason for the sand is to level out 1/4" or so imperfections in the base stone. if you were to put the slabs directly onto the base some of them would snap like a branch over your knee.

    go buy some 1" outside diameter black pipe (found at HomeDepot, commonally used for indoor gas lines). at a minimum, get 3 10'ers and have one cut in half. these will be your screed rails.

    Jim, a lot of guys use stone dust or screenings as a leveling agent. Try and get your base as close as possible, then make minor adjustments with the screenings. You could also go the approach of getting a few shorter screed poles and after leveling the poles, you can screed an inch of screenings all at once, then lay your block.

    Since you don't have a laser, just level your first block, front to back and side to side (use a torpedo level or two footer). When you set your next block, use your already leveled block to level the adjacent block. Work your way down the first course and use a four foot level to check the level on 4 or 5 block at a time. Check to see that the level is also touching on every block at the same time and there are none that are too low. Follow this method all the way around and your last block should be the same height as your first.
